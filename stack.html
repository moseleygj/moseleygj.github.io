The Goal

My goal is to achieve a focused, distraction-free state using a minimal toolset, while maintaining the flexibility to spin up any environment I need via VMs, SSH, or remote desktop.


Over time, I’ve realized that no single operating system does everything perfectly. macOS is a polished Unix environment but falls short for gaming. Windows offers near-universal compatibility but is notoriously resource-intensive. Linux provides a fast, low-overhead development environment but can lack out-of-the-box support for certain proprietary drivers and software.


This is why I choose Fedora. Having installed almost every major Linux distribution over the years—including legacy, CLI-only distros—I’ve learned a valuable lesson: while compiling and configuring everything from scratch is a great flex, an unexpected bug can instantly derail your workflow. You end up spending hours troubleshooting the OS instead of doing the actual work you set out to do. Fedora simply gets out of the way and lets you focus on the build.
